About Me

Hello! I am Aravindan Kamatchi Sundaram, an interdisciplinary dual-degree student (B.Tech in Metallurgical and Materials Engineering + M.Tech in Data Science) at the Indian Institute of Technology Madras. I am currently pursuing my final year thesis in Adobe under the guidance of Aishwarya Agarwal and Dr. Balaji Vasan Srinivasan. Previously, I had worked for two years in the Materials Informatics Lab at IIT Madras under the guidance of Dr. Rohit Batra; first as a Young Research Fellow and later as an undergraduate researcher. In addition, I have completed two research internships at Adobe's Multimodal Intelligence Lab, mentored by Dr. Balaji Vasan Srinivasan, Dr. Srikrishna Karanam, and Aishwarya Agarwal.

Broadly, I am interested in exploring applications of generative AI and developing computational tools to accelerate materials discovery. As part of my two internships at Adobe's Multimodal Intelligence Labs, I have worked on improving text-to-image synthesis in diffusion models and domain specialization of multimodal large language models for improved design layout understanding. In the Materials Informatics Lab at IIT Madras, I have worked on developing an LLM-based workflow for constructing a high-entropy alloy database that can aid computational materials discovery.

I am interested in pursuing a Ph.D. and am looking for labs and research groups that work on pushing the boundaries of generative AI for various applications particularly in Computer Vision and Materials Science. If our interests align or you want to have a discussion, do connect!
